Our Legacy ↓

Over the years, the Mumbai Indians have etched a remarkable legacy in the annals of Indian cricket, exemplifying resilience, dominance, and unwavering commitment to excellence. With an illustrious history marked by multiple Indian Premier League (IPL) titles, they've consistently raised the bar for cricketing standards.

Beyond their on-field exploits, the Mumbai Indians have cultivated a culture of nurturing young talent, fostering a pipeline of future cricketing stars. Their success isn't just measured by trophies but by their enduring impact on the cricketing landscape, inspiring generations of fans and setting new benchmarks for sporting greatness.

Moreover, their commitment to community initiatives and philanthropic endeavors underscores their ethos of giving back and making a meaningful difference beyond the boundary ropes. In essence, the legacy of the Mumbai Indians transcends mere cricketing achievements; it's a testament to their indomitable spirit and enduring legacy in the realm of Indian cricket.

  • 2013

    Champions

    Most runs: Rohit Sharma - 538 runs
    Most wickets: Harbhajan Singh - 24 wickets

    In 2013, the Mumbai Indians clinched their maiden Indian Premier League (IPL) trophy, beating Chennai Super Kings, marking a historic moment in the franchise's journey, led by the astute leadership of Rohit Sharma.

  • 2015

    Champions

    Most runs: Lendl Simmons - 540 runs
    Most wickets: Lasith Malinga - 24 wickets

    In 2015, the Mumbai Indians secured their second Indian Premier League (IPL) championship, triumphing over Chennai Super Kings once again in a thrilling finale

  • 2017

    Champions

    Most runs: Parthiv Patel - 395 runs
    Most wickets: Jasprit Bumrah - 20 wickets

    In 2017, the Mumbai Indians scripted yet another glorious chapter in their IPL saga by clinching their third title in a nail-biting finale against Rising Pune Supergiant.

  • 2019

    Champions

    Most runs: Quinton de Kock - 529 runs
    Most wickets: Jasprit Bumrah - 19 wickets

    In 2019, the Mumbai Indians reaffirmed their dominance in the Indian Premier League (IPL) by clinching their fourth title in a thrilling final against the Chennai Super Kings.

  • 2020

    Champions

    Most runs: Ishan Kishan - 516 runs
    Most wickets: Jasprit Bumrah - 27 wickets

    In 2020, amidst unprecedented challenges and uncertainties, the Mumbai Indians emerged as the undisputed champions of the Indian Premier League (IPL), claiming their fifth title in a fiercely competitive season.

Our Legends

  • Lasith Malinga

    Lasith Malinga, the Sri Lankan pace maestro, played a pivotal role for the Mumbai Indians in the IPL. Renowned for his deadly yorkers, he clinched crucial victories, notably in the IPL 2019 final with a nerve-wracking last over. Malinga's leadership and experience also guided younger players, leaving an indelible mark on the franchise's history.

  • Kieron Pollard

    Kieron Pollard, the powerhouse from the West Indies, has been a cornerstone for the Mumbai Indians. Known for his explosive batting and match-winning performances, Pollard's towering sixes and astute captaincy have steered the team to numerous victories, making him a fan favorite and a vital asset for the franchise.

  • Sachin Tendulkar

    Sachin Tendulkar, the legendary Indian batsman, was the heart and soul of the Mumbai Indians. As the team's icon player and former captain, Tendulkar's unparalleled cricketing wisdom, masterful strokes, and unwavering commitment inspired generations. His presence in the squad instilled confidence and elevated the team's performance, leaving an enduring legacy in the annals of Mumbai Indians' history.
